Supporting Student Learning with AI

Research-Based Framework

Current research identifies five key strategies for effectively integrating AI in teaching:

1. Multiple Examples

Help students understand complex concepts through varied examples and contexts

2. Varied Explanations

Address different learning styles and levels of understanding

3. Misconception Addressing

Identify and correct common misunderstandings early

4. Low-Stakes Testing

Frequent opportunities for self-assessment and feedback

5. Distributed Practice

Spread learning and reinforcement across time for better retention

Step 1: Choose a Course Concept

Think of a concept from your course that students often find challenging. Consider:

  • Topics where students frequently ask for additional explanations
  • Concepts that benefit from multiple examples
  • Ideas that build on previous knowledge

Step 2: Explore Student AI Usage

Current research shows how students use AI for learning:

Common Student Uses

  • Writing support (36%)
  • Brainstorming (29%)
  • Understanding concepts (20%)
  • Research assistance (14%)

Faculty Concerns

  • Critical thinking impact
  • Information accuracy
  • Skill development
  • Academic integrity

Explore Integration Strategies

Use the AI chat to explore how students might use AI to understand your chosen concept. Consider both:

Productive Uses

  • Getting varied explanations
  • Finding relevant examples
  • Practice problem-solving
  • Self-assessment

Potential Pitfalls

  • Bypassing understanding
  • Over-reliance
  • Missing key context
  • Accepting errors
