Workshop Summary

Reflecting on key insights and planning for continued implementation of AI-enhanced teaching.

Research Foundation Key Takeaways Implementation Steps Teaching Resources

Key Takeaways

Understanding AI's Role

  • AI as learning companion and tool
  • Balance of benefits and limitations
  • Importance of guided implementation

Supporting Student Learning

  • Multiple examples and explanations
  • Addressing misconceptions
  • Low-stakes practice opportunities

Course Design Strategies

  • Authentic assessment approaches
  • Constructive alignment principles
  • Integration with learning outcomes

Working with AI

  • Iterative refinement process
  • Building on initial responses
  • Continuous dialogue approach
  • Progressive improvement

Implementation Support

  • Clear guidelines and policies
  • Student guidance frameworks
  • Continuous improvement process

Research Foundation

This workshop draws from current research on AI in education:

Teaching Strategies & AI Integration

  • Mollick & Mollick (2023) - Five evidence-based strategies for implementing AI in teaching, including multiple examples, varied explanations, and addressing misconceptions.
  • Stöhr et al. (2024) - Study of student AI usage and attitudes across disciplines, highlighting adoption patterns and pedagogical implications.

Academic Integrity & Assessment

  • Latest Insights Report (2024) - Current data on student and faculty perspectives, showing 45% student adoption vs 15% faculty adoption of AI tools.
  • Dai et al. (2023) - Framework for reconceptualizing AI as student-driven innovation in higher education.
